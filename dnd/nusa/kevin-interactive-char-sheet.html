Monk Ki Features
Flurry of Blows
Attack Bonus 1 Ki

After you take the Attack action, make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action. Each is +7 to hit, 1d6 + 4 bludgeoning.

With Extra Attack, the standard combo is: Attack (2 strikes) → Flurry (2 more strikes) = four attacks per turn. Drunken Technique also triggers: you gain the benefit of Disengage and +10 ft speed this turn.

Stunning Strike Monk 5
Attack Action 1 Ki

When you hit another creature with a melee weapon attack, spend 1 ki to force the target to make a CON save DC 14 or be stunned until the end of your next turn.

Stunned = incapacitated, can't move, auto-fails STR/DEX saves, attacks against it have advantage. The single highest-impact ki spend in the book — save it for bosses or casters. Can be triggered on any melee hit, not limited to unarmed strikes.

Patient Defense
Bonus 1 Ki

Take the Dodge action as a bonus action: attacks against you have disadvantage, you make DEX saves with advantage.

Pair with Stunning Strike turns where you want to lock a target down without attacking again, or as a panic button when low HP.

Step of the Wind
Bonus 1 Ki

As a bonus action, take the Dash or Disengage action. Jump distance is doubled this turn.

Long jump = 24 ft. High jump = 8 ft (clears most Medium creatures).

Celestial Revelation — Radiant Soul Aasimar 3 · PB / LR
Bonus 3 / LR

Bonus action to transform for 1 minute. While transformed:

• Sprout wings — fly speed 30 ft
• Once on each of your turns, add +5 radiant damage (your level) to one attack or damaging spell.

The build's defining move. At Monk 5 with Extra Attack + Flurry, a typical transformed turn is: bonus action to transform (turn 1 only) → Attack × 2 → Flurry × 2. One of those four strikes adds +5 radiant. Plus 30 ft of flight to reach anything.

3 uses per long rest. Can end early as a bonus action on a later turn.

Monk Defense
Deflect Missiles
Reaction

When hit by a ranged weapon attack, reduce damage by 1d10 + 9 (1d10 + DEX + Monk level).

If reduced to 0 and the missile is small enough to hold one-handed (with a hand free), you catch it. Spend 1 ki as part of the same reaction to throw it back: ranged attack +7, 20/60 ft.

Throwback damage = the caught missile's own damage die + DEX mod (arrow / crossbow bolt = 1d8 + 4, sling bullet = 1d4 + 4, dart = 1d4 + 4). Not a flat 1d10.

A pure monk drunken brawler with a celestial twist. No shield, no heavy weapons — just fists, feet, and Radiant Soul. Operates in two gears: normal (the default stance) and transformed (Radiant Soul active for a minute, 3×/LR).

Normal Stance

Unarmored monk — Martial Arts, Flurry, Stunning Strike, Drunken Technique.
AC 17
Speed 40 ft
Flurry Speed 50 ft
Unarmed 1d6 + 4
Hands Both free

Standard damage turn: Attack (2 strikes via Extra Attack) → Flurry of Blows (2 more) → Disengage & walk 50 ft via Drunken Technique. Four attacks at +7 for 1d6+4 each, with Disengage and a move baked in.

Lockdown turn: Attack → on hit, spend 1 ki for Stunning Strike (CON save DC 14). Can attempt on every melee hit this turn — keep trying until it lands or ki runs out. A stunned boss loses its next turn and all attackers get advantage.

Best when: most fights. This is your default — don't burn Radiant Soul on random bandits.

Transformed Stance — Radiant Soul

Wings unfurl. Golden light flickers around the knuckles. 1 minute, bonus action to enter.
AC 17
Fly Speed 30 ft
Walk Speed 40 ft
Unarmed 1d6 + 4 (+5 rad once/turn)
Duration 1 min

Opener: Bonus action to transform (turn 1 only) → Attack × 2 → Flurry × 2. One of those four strikes adds +5 radiant damage (your Monk level).

Mobility: 30 ft of flight. Reach flying enemies, cross chasms, drop on casters from above (with Slow Fall as insurance if the duration lapses mid-air).

Best when: boss fight, undead or fiends (radiant is doubly nasty against both), or you need to get somewhere fast and the terrain says no. 3 uses per long rest — so roughly one per encounter you care about.

The celestial nature is usually quiet — the drunken style reads as a normal, shabby brawler. Radiant Soul is the reveal: when the wings come out, the illusion is done.

Identity

Name: Ren (birth name: Liraz)

Species / Age / Height: Aasimar · age · height

Alignment: —

Liraz left the monastery. The town gave him a new name, Ren, and stopped asking about the old one.

Known As — the town's chant
"Ren won't drink without friend."

Half a rule, half a joke, half an invitation. The townsfolk started it, and it stuck — he really won't drink alone, and the regulars know it. Call it out in any tavern he's been in and someone will finish the line for you.

Flavor — the concept in one line

"A celestial monk whose drunken style masks the radiant power within."

The outside: shabby robes, unsteady gait, a smile that says he's on his third cup. The inside: trained harder than most of you have ever trained, carries a celestial lineage he doesn't talk about, and walked out on the order that wanted to forge him into something sharper.

Personality Traits

• Shows up for the underdog. If someone smaller is getting worked over, his whole posture changes. The drunk act falls away fast when it needs to.

• Won't drink alone. Solitude reminds him too much of the monastery. A cup by himself is just meditation with extra steps.

Ideals

• Protection. "Discipline that breaks children isn't discipline. I won't stand by for that again."

• Freedom. "I spent years inside walls that decided what I was allowed to want. No more."

Bonds

• The boy at the monastery. The initiate he mentored, left behind when he fled. Thinks of him most days. Tells himself he left to get strong enough to come back for him.

• The townsfolk who took him in. The first people in his adult life who wanted nothing from him except his company. He'd burn a lot down before he let harm come to them.

• A former friend at the order. — TBD; one of the fellow senior students he was close to, still inside. Does that friend understand why he left?

Flaws

• Regret he won't admit to. He left people behind — a kid especially — and he tells himself a story about why that doesn't quite hold up when he's sober.

• The drunk act can stop being an act. One cup for cover becomes four because four cups make the regret go quieter.

• Avoidance of the monastery. Won't go near it. Won't speak its name. If the plot drags him toward it, expect him to push back.

Physical Description

— Common clothes that have seen a lot of spilled arak. Hair that hasn't been cut by a monk's razor in a while. The Aasimar tell is subtle most of the time — faintly luminous at the eyes in low light, or a warmth to the skin a hair past what's natural. Only obvious when Radiant Soul fires and the wings unfold.

Full Backstory

Shelter of the Faithful (Acolyte): there's still a temple that considers him one of their own, even if he won't admit it's the same monastery he walked out of. Open plot thread: what happens when the order sends someone to bring him back — or sends the kid to bring him back?
